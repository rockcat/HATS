# DivaTech — Trainer Recruitment Pipeline
**Deliverable: TKT-011 | Author: Casey | Status: Complete**

---

## Pipeline Philosophy

We are not posting a job ad and waiting. The kind of trainer we need — culturally fluent, emotionally intelligent, credible on AI, experienced in corporate settings — is unlikely to be actively job-hunting. They are busy, in-demand, and probably already delivering for someone else. **We go to them. We don't wait for them to come to us.**

This is a relationship-first pipeline, not a transactional one.

## Outreach Approach

### Principles
- Lead with the mission — people who care about responsible AI adoption will respond
- Be specific about why we're contacting *them* — reference something real
- Be honest about where we are — early-stage, building something new
- Name the MENA context upfront — let candidates self-select appropriately

### Message Framework
> *"We're building a small, specialist cohort of trainers to deliver GenAI literacy programmes to corporate audiences — our first engagement is with a major financial institution in the MENA region. We came across your background in [specific experience] and felt you might be exactly the kind of facilitator we're looking for. This isn't a volume play — we're recruiting 3–5 people who care about how AI lands with real humans in real organisations. Would you be open to a conversation?"*

## Pipeline Stages

### Stage 1 — Outreach & Expression of Interest
- Targeted outreach via channels above
- Short EOI form (low friction — we're approaching busy professionals)
- EOI captures: current facilitation work, AI familiarity, MENA/financial services experience, availability and rate expectations
- **Human signal**: how do they talk about learners? Do they centre the room or their own expertise?

### Stage 2 — Screening Conversation (30 mins)
- Warm and conversational — not an interrogation
- Explore: their relationship with AI, experience in emotionally charged rooms, cultural awareness and adaptability
- Share DivaTech mission and MENA context honestly — two-way assessment
- **Human signal**: do they ask good questions about the learners? Do they lean in when we mention AI anxiety?

### Stage 3 — Practical Facilitation Audition (30 mins)
- Non-negotiable — room presence and emotional intelligence cannot be assessed on paper
- Format: 20-minute facilitated segment on a provided GenAI topic + 10-minute debrief
- Observed by at least two DivaTech team members
- Assessment criteria from TKT-010 profile: GenAI fluency, facilitation quality, psychological safety, cultural adaptability, response to a planted "difficult participant" moment
- **Human signal**: how do they handle the moment something doesn't land? Grace under pressure or flustered?

### Stage 4 — Values & Fit Conversation
- Informal and exploratory
- Covers: relationship with feedback, handling failure in the room, what excites them about this work
- Handbook and quality standards introduced honestly — no surprises post-hire
- Confirm practical details: rates, availability, contractual approach
- **Human signal**: are they excited about the mission or just the engagement?

### Stage 5 — Offer & Onboarding
- Conditional offer issued
- Onboarding per TKT-012 trainer handbook
- **Target cohort: 3–5 trainers ready before first client delivery**

## Rates & Contracting

- **Expected day rates**: £600–£1,200/day (UK market); potentially higher for in-country Gulf delivery. Morgan to validate before offers go out.
- **Contracting model**: Freelance associate — standard in L&D, gives flexibility as we scale
- **Contract must cover**: IP ownership, confidentiality (critical for financial services clients), any exclusivity provisions, quality standards expectations
- **Transparency principle**: Be honest with candidates about pipeline stage — we're building toward a first delivery, not pretending we have a full programme ready
